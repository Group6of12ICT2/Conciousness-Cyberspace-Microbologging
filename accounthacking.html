Account Hacking
Account hacking is a sort of cyberattack in which a hacker exploits the computer weaknesses to gets an access o to gain unauthorized personal or organizational data.

How to avoid being a victim of Accoung Hacking?

  • Use Multi-Factor Authentication.
  • Use a Password manager.
  • Create a Strong Password.
  • Keep tabs on what sites may be compromised
  • Update and Encrypt Everything
  • Remove All of Your Digital Footprint.

How would you determine if you're a victim of account hacking?

  • You get a ransomware message.
  • You get a fake antivirus message.
  • You have unwanted browser toolbars.
  • Your internet searches are redirected.
  • You see frequent, random popups.
  • Your friends receive social media invitations from you that you didn’t send.
  • Your online password isn’t working.
  • You observe unexpected software installs.
  • Your mouse moves between programs and makes selections.
  • Antimalware, Task Manager or Registry Editor is disabled.
  • Your online account is missing money.
  • You’ve been notified by someone you’ve been hacked.
  • Confidential data has been leaked.
  • Your credentials are in a password dump.
  • You observe strange network traffic patterns.

What do you do and who do you contact if you're a victim of Account Hacking?

  • If your social profile is hacked, use the Social's Help Community to report the incident. The compromised account will be shut down as they investigate the matter.
  • Freeze your credit, block compromised accounts.
  • Scan your devices for malware.
  • Secure your wifi network.
