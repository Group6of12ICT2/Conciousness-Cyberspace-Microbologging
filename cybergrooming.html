Cyber Grooming
Cyber grooming is the act of developing a trustworthy relationship with a minor online in order to commit a sexual misconduct. "Common interests" can help malefactors get the attention of teens: discussing fashion, art, sports, music, and entertainment. or use bullying and trolling (provocations, harassment, psychological manipulation) to act aggressively.

How do you avoid cyber grooming?

  • Website management and blocking.
    Blocking and managing websites is another way to prevent cyberbullying and online predators. You can control which websites children can visit. Limiting your child's freedom and preventing them from accessing harmful websites can go a long way toward protecting them. You can blacklist websites that you do not want your children to visit because many browsers come equipped with built-in security.
  • Monitoring a child's online activity.
    You don't have to constantly keep an eye on your child because of this. Ask them periodically to demonstrate what they are doing and with whom they are talking to. It's always a good idea to talk openly with your kids about the danger posed by strangers online. You will need to believe what they say when you investigate the situation because trust is a two-way street.

How would you identify the Cyber Groomer?

  • Someone send you lots of messages.
  • Ask you to keep your conversations secret.
  • Try to find out more.
  • Start sending you sexual messages.
  • Get you to share personal information.
  • Try to blackmail you.
